➡️ Follow the steps below to make your accounts and devices safer.

Start the Quick Checklist

How to Protect

1. Use strong, unique passwords

How: Choose passphrases (3+ unrelated words) or use a password manager.

Do this now: Install a password manager and change one reused password.

2. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)

How: Use authenticator apps or hardware keys (avoid SMS if possible).

Do this now: Turn on 2FA for your email and social accounts.

3. Keep software up to date

How: Enable automatic updates on phone/PC and apps.

Do this now: Check for system updates and install.

4. Recognize and avoid phishing

How: Don’t click unknown links; hover to check URLs; verify sender.

Do this now: Take a screenshot and report suspicious links.

5. Secure networks

How: Use WPA2/3 Wi-Fi with strong router password; avoid public Wi-Fi or use VPN.

Do this now: Change your router’s default password.

6. Lock & encrypt devices

How: Enable device passcodes, biometrics, and disk encryption.

Do this now: Turn on screen lock with PIN or biometric.

7. Back up important data

How: Use encrypted cloud or offline backups.

Do this now: Create a backup of your most important files.

8. Limit what you share on social media

How: Remove sensitive info (phone, address, birthdays).

Do this now: Review privacy settings and remove one risky post.

9. Review app permissions & connected apps

How: Revoke access for apps you no longer use.

Do this now: Remove one suspicious app.

10. Teach others & report incidents

How: Share guidance with family; report fraud to platforms.

Do this now: Forward this checklist to a friend.

Real Social Media Examples

Phishing DM: “Urgent: Your account will be closed. Click here to verify: http://bit.ly/verify-now”

⚠️ Red flags: urgent tone, short link.

✅ Action: Don’t click. Screenshot & report.

Fake Friend Request: “Hi, I lost my old account — send me $50?”

⚠️ Red flags: money request, new account.

✅ Action: Verify offline, don’t send money.

Clickbait Link: “Wow this video of you is everywhere! http://short.link”

⚠️ Red flags: unexpected tag, short link.

✅ Action: Ignore, delete, report.
